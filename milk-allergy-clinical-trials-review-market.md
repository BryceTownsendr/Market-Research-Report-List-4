<p><p>Milk allergy clinical trials typically involve four main types of tests: Skin Prick Tests, Blood Allergen Specific IgE Tests, Oral Food Challenge, and Food Elimination Diet. Skin Prick Tests involve pricking the skin with a small amount of milk protein to see if there is a reaction. Blood Allergen Specific IgE Tests measure the levels of milk-specific antibodies in the blood. Oral Food Challenge involves consuming small amounts of milk to observe for allergic reactions. Food Elimination Diet involves removing milk from the diet to see if symptoms improve.</p></p>
