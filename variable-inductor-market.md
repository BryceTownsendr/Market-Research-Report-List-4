<p><p>Variable inductors are commonly used in electronic circuits to adjust the inductance value. Ordinary variable inductors are more cost-effective and are generally used in applications where accuracy is not critical. On the other hand, precision variable inductors are designed to provide highly accurate and stable inductance values, making them suitable for applications that require precise control over the inductance. Precision variable inductors are typically more expensive due to their high level of precision and are commonly used in industries such as telecommunications and aerospace.</p></p>
