<p><p>Non-contact liquid level sensors are devices used to detect the level of liquid in a container without physical contact with the liquid. Ultrasonic liquid level sensors use sound waves to measure the distance between the sensor and the liquid surface. Radar liquid level sensors use electromagnetic waves to determine the level of liquid. Both types of sensors offer advantages such as accurate measurements, reduced maintenance, and compatibility with various types of liquids, making them popular choices in industries such as manufacturing, chemical, and oil and gas.</p></p>
