<p><p>CAD/CAM milling machines for dental laboratories utilize two main types of processes: subtractive and additive. Subtractive processes involve removing material from a solid block to create the final restoration, while additive processes involve building up the restoration layer by layer. Subtractive processes are more commonly used in dental laboratories due to their accuracy and efficiency in producing high-quality restorations. Additive processes, on the other hand, are still evolving and gaining popularity in the dental industry for their ability to create complex designs with a high level of precision.</p></p>
