<p><p>Adrenoceptor blocking drugs are categorized into three main types: Alpha blockers, Beta blockers, and Others. Alpha blockers work by blocking the effects of adrenaline on alpha receptors in the body, leading to vasodilation and reduced blood pressure. Beta blockers block the effects of adrenaline on beta receptors, primarily in the heart, resulting in decreased heart rate and blood pressure. The Others category includes drugs that target other adrenoceptors or have a combination of alpha and beta-blocking effects. These drugs are commonly used to treat hypertension, heart conditions, and certain neurological disorders.</p></p>
