<p><p>Optical semiconductor devices can be categorized into two main types: Luminescent Devices and Light-receiving Devices. Luminescent devices, such as light-emitting diodes (LEDs) and laser diodes, emit light when an electric current passes through them. On the other hand, light-receiving devices, like photodiodes and phototransistors, detect and convert light signals into electrical signals. The market for these devices is driven by the increasing demand for energy-efficient lighting solutions and advancements in communication technology, leading to a growing need for high-performance optical semiconductor devices.</p></p>
