<p><p>Digital wound measurement devices are divided into two main types: contact type and non-contact type. Contact type devices require physical touch with the wound to measure its dimensions, while non-contact type devices utilize advanced technologies such as laser or imaging sensors to measure wounds without direct contact. Both types offer accurate and efficient wound measurements, with contact devices being more cost-effective and accessible, while non-contact devices are often preferred for their non-invasive nature and ability to capture detailed images of the wound.</p></p>
