<p><p>Oral thrush is a fungal infection that can manifest in different forms, such as pseudomembranous, erythematous, and hyperplastic. Pseudomembranous oral thrush appears as creamy white patches on the tongue and inner cheeks. Erythematous oral thrush causes redness and soreness in the mouth. Hyperplastic oral thrush results in white or gray raised lesions on the tongue or inside the cheeks. These different types of oral thrush may vary in appearance and severity, but they all require proper diagnosis and treatment.</p></p>
