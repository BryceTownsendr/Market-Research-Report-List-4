<p><p>Phone batteries are primarily classified into two types – Lithium Ion Battery and Nickel Metal Hydride Battery. Lithium Ion batteries are the most commonly used in phones due to their high energy density, lightweight, and long lifespan. They are also known for their quick charging capabilities. Nickel Metal Hydride batteries, on the other hand, are less common in phones nowadays but are still used in some older models. They are known for their lower energy density but are more environmentally friendly compared to lithium-ion batteries.</p></p>
