<p><strong>The Radio Telescope Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Continuous Aperture Radio Telescope</li><li>Discontinuous Aperture Radio Telescope</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Continuous aperture radio telescopes have a seamless, unbroken surface that collects radio waves from space without interruption. This design allows for a more consistent and accurate collection of data. Discontinuous aperture radio telescopes, on the other hand, have segmented surfaces with smaller panels that may not align perfectly, leading to potential gaps in the data collection. Despite this drawback, discontinuous aperture telescopes can be more cost-effective and easier to construct than continuous aperture telescopes.</p></p>
