<p><p>Cochlear implant systems are available as unilateral systems, involving implantation in one ear, or binaural systems, where implants are placed in both ears. Unilateral systems are typically used for individuals with profound hearing loss in one ear, while binaural systems are employed for those with bilateral hearing loss. Binaural systems offer advantages like improved sound localization and speech understanding in noisy environments. Both types of systems aim to effectively restore auditory function and improve quality of life for individuals with hearing impairment.</p></p>
