<p><p>Optical Emission Spectroscopy (OES) is a technique used for elemental analysis by measuring the light emitted from a sample when excited by a high-energy source. Arc/Spark OES is a type of OES where the sample is excited by an electric arc or spark, while Inductively Coupled Plasma (ICP) OES utilizes an inductively coupled plasma as the excitation source. Arc/Spark OES is often used for solid samples, while ICP OES is preferred for liquid samples due to its high sensitivity and ability to analyze multiple elements simultaneously.</p></p>
